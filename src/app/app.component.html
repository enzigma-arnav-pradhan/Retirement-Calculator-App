📘 Quick Start User Guide

Welcome to the Retirement Planner! Follow this step-by-step framework to map out your retirement roadmap in 5 minutes.

Step 1

Localize Your Plan

Select your target country from the dropdown at the top of the settings. This automatically sets currency formatting, inflation averages, tax estimates, and typical expected returns for your location.

Step 2

Define Your Timeline

Set your Current Age, planned Retirement Age, and conservative Life Expectancy. This defines your Accumulation Phase (investing years) and Decumulation Phase (retirement spending years).

Step 3

Input Expenses & Inflation

Enter your current monthly expense in today's money value. SWR (Safe Withdrawal Rate, default 4%) determines what corpus is needed to support this expense. We adjust this automatically to cover your location's **Retirement Tax Rate** and set aside an **Emergency Buffer** (e.g., 12 months of expenses).

Step 4

Map Out Your Savings & Investments

Input your existing retirement savings, any lump sums you can invest today, and your current monthly SIP amount. Keep the **Annual SIP Step-Up** (e.g., 5% - 10%) turned on to model how your monthly savings will grow as your salary increases over time. Use **Ad-hoc Investments** for one-off expected windfalls (like bonuses or inheritance).

Step 5

Review Results & Resolve Shortfalls

Look at the **KPI Dashboard** and **Wealth Advisor's Analysis**. If you have a shortfall, the calculator dynamically computes the **Required Monthly SIP** or the **One-time Lump Sum Today** you need to plug the gap. Inspect the chart trajectory and toggle the **Year-on-Year Ledger** tab to review the exact ledger.

💡 Key Terms Explained

Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR)
The estimated percentage of your retirement corpus you can withdraw annually without running out of money. The standard rule of thumb is 4%.
Emergency Buffer
A dedicated liquid reserve fund of 6–12 months of expenses, kept separate from volatile equity investments to prevent selling during market crashes.
Step-Up SIP / Contribution Step-Up
Increasing your monthly investment amount annually by a fixed percentage to keep pace with your career promotions and income growth.
Retirement Income Tax
Taxes deducted from withdrawals. We gross up your required withdrawals from the corpus to make sure your net take-home cash matches your actual monthly expenses.
